Hunters Spring provides a serene wintertime escape in the mountains of central Montana. The cabin is only available for rent from December through March, as it is used for Forest Service employees during warmer months. The roomy cabin makes a prime destination for snowmobile and skiing enthusiasts.

Access is by snowmobile, skis or snowshoes only. The road is not plowed.

drivingDirection:

From Harlowton, Montana, drive west for 32 miles on U.S. Highway 12 to Forest Road 274(Spring Creek Road). Take Forest Road 274 to mile marker 7 and park in the parking area. Proceed, via snowshoes, snowmobile or skis, another 6 miles, past Whitetail Camp, to Forest Road 6429. Turn right and go a 1/2 mile to Hunters Spring Guard Station.

facilitiesDescription:

The rustic two-story cabin sleeps up to eight guests in three roomy bedrooms. The kitchen is outfitted with a propane cook stove, refrigerator and a wood stove for heat. The dining room is furnished with a table and chairs. Firewood is provided for the wood stove and cooking pots and utensils are on hand in the kitchen.

No water, electricity or sewer is provided. Guests should bring plenty of water, bedding, food, warm clothing, flashlights, matches, and other basic camping items. ( Click here for more cabin details. )

note: '' orientationDescription:

Hunters Spring is located in the Little Belt Mountains near Whitetail Creek at an altitude of about 6,400 feet. Lodgepole pine and Douglas fir can be found in the area, and deer and elk are common.

recreationDescription:

Since the cabin is available in winter months only, featured activities include cross country skiing, snowmobiling and snowshoeing through snowy white scenery.
