Located at the beginning of a four-mile sand spit that separates the Pacific Ocean and Nehalem Bay. The park offers year-around family fun. Recreational opportunities includes hiking, biking, beachcombing, crabbing, clamming, horseback riding, and fishing. Horse Concession is now running year round at the park . www,oregonbeachrides.com

Park has wind gusts that may move tents and other items that are not tacked down. Wind gusts come up occasionally. MAKE SURE YOUR SITE MEETS YOUR CAMPING NEEDS

Before reserving, please become familiar with the particulars of your chosen site, including the size of the tent pad area and the type of equipment that you may bring to the park. Site details and tent pad sizes can be viewed online by clicking on the site name.

Directions to Nehalem Bay State Park Turn off US 101 at the Shell station between Manzanita and Nehalem. Travel 1 1/2 miles to the park. Follow signs to the campground 1/2 mile inside park entrance.
