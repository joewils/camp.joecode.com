Al Taylor Cabin is located adjacent to Steel Creek near the Continental Divide, at an elevation of 6,300 feet.

Visitors come to this area for its beautiful scenery and solitude.

drivingDirection:

The cabin can be accessed by traveling west from Kilgore, Idaho, via Forest Road 006 approximately 8 miles to Forest Road 478.

Travel Forest Road 478 to the Steel Creek Group Area.

The cabin is located just outside of the group area and can be reached by driving through the group area to the cabin.

The cabin can also be reached from I-15 at Stoddard Creek Exit.

Exit at the Stoddard Creek Exit and travel east on Forest Road 006 approximately 13 miles to Forest Road 478.

facilitiesDescription:

The cabin can accommodate six people. The cabin has a wood cook stove and wood fireplace as well as propane lights. It provides a small dining table with four chairs. The beds are wooden bunk beds. Other amenities include a fire ring, charcoal grill, picnic tables and a vault toilet - all provided adjacent to the cabin. There is no electricity or water at the cabin.

facility: AL TAYLOR CABIN fullReservationUrl: http://www.reserveamerica.com/campsiteSearch.do?contractCode=NRSO&parkId=115789&cmp=39-32--joewils importantInformation:

Pick up the key to the cabin at the Ranger District Office in Dubois, Idaho. Bears and wolves frequent the area; be aware; keep a clean camp. Store food and garbage in closed containers. Steel Creek Group Area is located adjacent to the cabin. For more information contact the Dubois Ranger District at 208-374-5422.

nearbyAttrctionDescription:

There are many miles of off-highway vehicle, horseback and hiking trails in the area. The Camas Meadows Battlefield located on the Nez Perce National Historic Trail is located nearby.

note: '' orientationDescription:

The cabin is surrounded by large douglas fir trees. It is near both Camas Meadows and the Continental Divide, providing incredible views.

recreationDescription:

An off-highway vehicle trail system can be accessed from the cabin. Small streams in the area provide fishing opportunities for brook trout. Other popular activities in the area include hiking, horseback riding and sightseeing.
