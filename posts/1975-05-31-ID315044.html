City of Rocks is a unique geologic area exhibiting granite pinnacles and monoliths in excess of sixty stories tall.

It has long been an oddity and wonder, especially for passing emigrants of the California Trail (1843-1869).

Today the park offers camping, hiking, backpacking, site-seeing, hunting, equestrian riding, mountain biking, exceptional rock climbing and much more.

drivingDirection:

From Boise and the west take l-84 to exit 216 (Declo); go south on Idaho 77 to Conner Creek Junction, then southwest on the Elba-Almo road to the visitor center in Almo and the park entrance.

From Pocatello and Idaho Falls, take l-86 and l-84 to exit 216 and proceed as above.

From Salt Lake City take l-84 to exit 245 (Sublett/Malta) then head west toward Malta, Elba, and Almo.
